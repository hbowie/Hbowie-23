3. Fair and Inclusive Decision-Making

↓ ↑ ⌂
  • Everyone gets a chance to participate in decision-making
  • Decisions are made in a way recognized by all to be fair
  • Decisions are made at the lowest possible level:
    • to confer a sense of empowerment,
    • to allow decisions to be made based on local circumstances
  • Participation can take various forms. As examples:
    • Pure democracy: everyone gets a vote
    • Everyone gets a veto
    • Everyone has a chance to be heard
    • Everyone has a chance to voice objections
  • Once made, fairly made decisions must be respected by all
