Michael Pisaro: “The voice is infinitely nuanced”

↓ ↑ ⌂

The voice is infinitely nuanced — at times almost an authoritarian monotone (not unlike Ginsberg reading “Howl”), at times compassionate, tragic — but also angry, vengeful, gleeful, ironic, weary, spectral, haranguing. And it would sound this way in ancient Greek or contemporary Russian. There is so much desire and so much power in this voice….

Michael Pisaro, composer, speaking about Dylan's recording of "Like A Rolling Stone"
