3. Recordings as Creative Input

↓ ↑ ⌂
  • Previous generations of musicians learned from:
    • Live music, teachers, mentors, written music
  • These musicians tended to work within certain traditions, often bound by geography
  • Starting in the early sixties, musicians began learning primarily from recordings
    • And from books and films as well
  • This enabled what became known as “The British Invasion”
  • This empowered musical creators to draw from, and combine:
    • Any sources that inspired them
    • Any musical elements that would serve a particular song
